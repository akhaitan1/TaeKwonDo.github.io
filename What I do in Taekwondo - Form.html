What I do in Taekwondo - Form    
 
       
Navigation  

Forms are a sequence of moves that a student is required to learn in order to move up to the next grade. 


There is at least one form per belt. At each level, you either learn a new form or you learn parts of a form. For example, at Green belt level 1, you learn the first part of your form but at Green belt level 2 you learn the second half. At white belt you learn the entire form (unless you are a Tiger Cub where you learn parts of the form at different belt levels). Yellow belts until Brown belts learn their form in three sections, depending on their level. At Red belt level 1, you learn one form and at level 2, you learn a different form. Then at level 3 you do both forms. At black belt, the first two levels have the same form that you learn in two pieces. Levels 3 and 4 do a different form and learn that in two pieces. 


Personally, my favourite form is the blue belt form (Won-Hyo).


In competitions, form is usually one section of it. You first do your form and then you are given ranks out of 5 by at least three judges based on your performance. In my opinion, this category is the easiest to win a medal.


Taekwondo forms teach your body good balance by executing kicks, blocks, and punches with power, but then maintaining proper posture as well as foot and hand position.
