What is Taekwondo
 
     
Navigation  

Taekwondo is a form of empty hand martial arts combat that requires the athlete to use all parts of the body in competition. Taekwondo is a self defense discipline; the name is derived from the Korean words "tae", meaning kick, "kwon", a punch or other blow with the hand or fist, and "do", which is the way so Taekwondo translates to ‘the way of the foot and fist’. Taekwondo is distinguished from other martial arts by its high speed, sweeping kicks and emphatic punches. The series of belt colours are as follows:

White belts don’t have a level unless you are a Tiger Cub (3 to 6 years old). In this case, white belts have seven levels. Yellow belts usually have three levels unless you are a Tiger Cub in which case, there are also seven levels. In belts Green to Red, there are 3 levels. Every Black belt degree has 4 levels.
